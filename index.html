Layer 3 · PassiveStart small, start now
Open a TFSA and start automated investing — even $10/month
Time in the market matters more than the amount. Start now.
The Tax-Free Savings Account is one of the most powerful tools available to Canadians and one of the most underused. Any growth inside it — dividends, capital gains — is completely tax-free. Even $25/month in a low-cost index ETF (like XEQT or VEQT) starts the compounding clock. The amount matters less than the habit. I can increase contributions as my income grows.

Layer 3 · PassiveGrowth
Grow TFSA to the point where it earns $100/month in dividends or growth
This is the first "money making money" threshold.
At a 5% average annual return, $24,000 invested generates roughly $100/month. This isn't the goal — it's a waypoint that makes the abstract feel real. Dividends from index funds and ETFs get reinvested automatically (DRIP), which accelerates compounding. The feeling of money arriving without any action on my part is qualitatively different and worth experiencing early.

Layer 4 · Reduce expensesOngoing
Audit subscriptions and recurring costs — cut anything unused
$200/month saved = $200 earned, but without the tax.
Reducing expenses is mathematically identical to earning more income, but it's after-tax — which makes it more powerful dollar for dollar. A $200/month cut to unnecessary costs is worth more than a $200 side income because that side income gets taxed. This doesn't mean deprivation — it means being intentional. I audit once a year and cut anything that doesn't actively add value to my life or work.

Layer 4 · Reduce expensesAnnual
Negotiate or switch: insurance, phone plan, internet
These rarely decrease on their own. Asking takes 20 minutes.
Most people overpay for recurring services because switching costs feel high. In reality, calling to cancel or switching providers once a year typically saves $200–600/year on phone alone. I set a recurring calendar reminder to review these annually — phone plan, internet, renter's or home insurance. The 20 minutes of discomfort is worth it every single time.
